Design Document: TPT DataCenter
Project Name: TPT DataCenter (Open-Source Data Center Infrastructure & Physical AI)
Industry: Data Center Facility Management & Hyperscale Infrastructure
Status: Phase 1 Design / Pre-Alpha
1. Executive Summary
AI data centers consume 3% of global electricity and are projected to hit 8% by 2030. Yet the physical infrastructure—power distribution, cooling, and facility management—is controlled by legacy giants (Schneider Electric, Vertiv, Nlyte) running software from the early 2000s. These systems are dumb, reactive, and waste megawatts of power.
TPT DataCenter is an open-source, AI-driven physical plant controller for hyperscale facilities. It replaces proprietary firmware in UPS systems, PDUs, and cooling units with memory-safe Rust agents. It ingests millions of sensor data points per second via Go telemetry pipelines. It uses reinforcement learning to predict thermal hotspots and dynamically optimize cooling, saving 20-30% in energy costs.
2. System Architecture
Core Components:
The Real-Time Facility Edge (Rust): Bare-metal Rust agents running directly on microcontrollers inside PDUs, UPS battery management systems (BMS), and cooling valves. They execute deterministic control loops, replacing proprietary C firmware.
The Telemetry Ingestion Engine (Go): A massively concurrent Go pipeline ingesting millions of data points (temperature, voltage, amperage, airflow) per second via Redfish, Modbus TCP, and IPMI, replacing legacy SNMP polling.
The Thermal AI Brain: A reinforcement learning model mapping the physical thermodynamics of the data center. It predicts hotspots before they happen and dynamically adjusts liquid cooling flow rates and fan speeds.
Hardware Management API: Full Redfish and IPMI implementation, allowing TPT DataCenter to talk to compute servers (Dell, HPE, Supermicro) and throttle CPU power dynamically when the grid is stressed.
The Physical Topology Graph: A Knowledge Graph representing every rack, PDU, cooling loop, and cable. The AI uses this graph to understand physical relationships and optimize holistically.
Target Tech Stack:
Edge/Firmware Language: Rust (bare-metal, no_std for UPS and cooling controllers)
Telemetry/Backend Language: Go (for ingesting massive time-series sensor data)
Protocols: Redfish (RESTful hardware management), Modbus TCP (for legacy chillers), IPMI, gRPC
Time-Series Database: QuestDB or InfluxDB (optimized for massive, high-frequency sensor writes)
AI/ML Framework: PyTorch or Ray (for training the thermal dynamics reinforcement learning models)
Hardware: Raspberry Pi Compute Module 4 or industrial ARM controllers for edge agents
3. Integration with TPT Ecosystem
TPT Dynamo (Power Source): Generates the power (solar/nuclear/grid)
TPT Relay (Power Distribution): Routes the power through substations
TPT DataCenter (Facility): Manages power inside the building, distributes to racks, and cools servers
TPT Aether (Network): Provides the 5G/6G connectivity that the data center hosts
The Link: DataCenter draws power from Relay, houses the GPUs running TPT Fulcrum, and uses Aether for external connectivity
4. Market Disruption
Hyperscalers & Colo Providers: Can cut cooling power bills by 30% using the Thermal AI Brain, saving millions per facility annually
Sovereign AI Clouds: Nations or enterprises building AI training clusters can manage the physical plant without paying Schneider Electric millions for EcoStruxure licenses
Ultimate Security: Because UPS and PDU firmware is open-source Rust, data centers can mathematically verify there are no state-sponsored backdoors in their power infrastructure
